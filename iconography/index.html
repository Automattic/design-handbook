Iconography

Icons carry a tremendous weight in conveying branding and design language. They give a UI it's most recognizable elements of identity when used correctly.

Right now we are in flux: In a persuit of an evolved icon set for WordPress.com. In the meantime, these are the available icon sets:

  • Noticons (note-icons, or not-icons) is our WordPress.com icon font. It contains every icon “Genericons” has, plus a couple of WordPress.com specific ones. You can use Genericons for themes and other projects. (Please do not use Noticons outside of their intended 16px font-size or 2x multiples thereof (32px, 64px). Even fonts look fuzzy outside of their pixel grid.)
  • Genericons is an open-source icon font. Most of it is included in Noticons. Useful for themes and non-WordPress.com stuff.
  • Dashicons is the official icon font of the WordPress admin as of 3.8.

If you'd like to join the icon revolution, head over to the Gridicons github repo, where the future of the WordPress.com icons is being crafted.
