Logotype

The WordPress logotype is composed of the "W" mark and type. Carefully crafted proportions and positioning of these two elements in relation to each other are the essential guideline to take from this page.

History

The WordPress logo was created in early 2005 by Jason Santa Maria for the WordPress open source core project. He used two Emigre typefaces for the logo: "Mrs. Eaves Small Caps" for the logotype, and "Dalliance" as influence for the "W" mark. The design was later visually refined by our very own Matt Miklic (formerly know as Matt Thomas, or MT) - the first designer at Automattic who helped shape the WordPress brand from very early on.

WordPress.com has used a variation of the core logotype since it's inception. In 2013, with the adoption of "Open Sans" as the text typeface, the logotype embraced it as well. We also decided to exclusively use the inverted "W" mark (a.k.a. the version without the outside ring) when representing WordPress.com.

Metrics

The metrics derive from the element that started it all: the inverted "W" mark. From there, a grid was set, defining all the other proportions. That includes the safety margin around the logotype - magic whitespace that protects our brand from the world. The safety margin should always be half the size of the "W" mark, unless otherwise specified.

The text has been carefully positioned and kerned to show the WordPress.com name in all its glory, so preferably always use a composite version of the logotype (in svg, other image format, or icon font), instead of typing the text next to the "W" mark.

More on this on the Metrics section of this Handbook.

Logotype + Titles

In some situations - like in this Design Handbook - there might be the need to add a Title paired with the Logotype. The metrics for that follow the same principles by which we positioned and sized the Logotype.

In the case where we need to reduce the logotype to just the "W" mark, the height of the title should correspond to the x height of the logotype word - equivalent to a small caps height version of Open Sans Semi-bold - but aligned to the middle axis of the "W" mark.
