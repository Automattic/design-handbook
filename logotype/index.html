Logotype

The WordPress.com logotype is composed of the “W” logo and “WordPress.com” name. Carefully crafted proportions and positioning of these two elements in relation to each other are the essential guidelines to take from this page.

History

The WordPress logo was created in early 2005 by Jason Santa Maria for the WordPress Open Source project. He used two Emigre typefaces: Mrs. Eaves Small Caps for the “WordPress” text, and Dalliance as influence for the “W” mark. The design was later visually refined by our very own Matt Miklic (formerly Matt Thomas, or MT) — the first designer at Automattic, who helped shape the WordPress brand from early on.

WordPress.com has used a variation of the core logotype since its inception. In 2013, with the adoption of Open Sans as the text typeface, the logotype embraced it as well. We also decided to exclusively use the simplified “W” mark (the version without the outer ring) when representing WordPress.com.

Metrics

The metrics derive from the element that started it all: the “W” mark. From there, a grid was set, defining all the other proportions. That includes the safety margin around the logotype — magic whitespace that protects our brand from the world. The safety margin should always be half the size of the “W” mark, unless otherwise specified.

The text has been carefully positioned and kerned to show the WordPress.com name in all its glory, so preferably always use a composite version of the logotype (in SVG, other image format, or icon font), instead of typing the text next to the “W” mark.

More on this on the Metrics section of this Handbook.

Logotype & Titles

In some situations — like in this Design Handbook — there might be the need to add a title paired with the logotype. The metrics for that follow the same principles by which we positioned and sized the logotype.

Titles should be set in Open Sans Semibold, all uppercase, with a height equivalent to the x-height of “WordPress.com” in the logotype. When a title is paired with the horizontal logotype, the title should sit on the same baseline as the logotype text.
