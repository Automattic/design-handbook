Typography

WordPress is all about spreading ideas and freedom of speech, specifically through the written word. On this page, we’ll explore the guidelines for consistent typography on WordPress.com.

Typefaces

WordPress.com uses two typefaces: Open Sans and Merriweather. These two faces, if used correctly, should be another vital element of our design language, giving a sense of familiarity to our users.

Open Sans

Open Sans is a humanist sans serif typeface designed by Steve Matteson, Type Director of Ascender Corp. Designed with an upright stress, open forms, and a neutral yet friendly appearance. It was optimized for print, web, and mobile interfaces, and has excellent legibility characteristics in its letterforms. It has inspired us greatly and the name itself aligns well with our values as a company.

Merriweather

This gracious typeface by Eben Sorkin was added to the Reader after many typesetting explorations. Eventually we all agreed Merriweather was the winner. Merriweather features a very large x-height, slightly condensed letterforms, a mild diagonal stress, sturdy serifs, and open forms. It should be used for post titles and potentially in some blockquotes.

Weights and Styles

We are very specific as to what weights to use of our glorious typefaces. Only in this way can we ensure that our design language is consistent. We use multiple weights from Open Sans: Light, Regular, Semibold, and Bold. As for Merriweather, we stick to the Bold weight.

Typographic Modular Scale

A harmonic ratio helps in creating a more harmonious design. If we use the same scale across WordPress.com, things feel more cohesive — it’s as much about consistency as it is about harmony. Instead of using arbitrary numbers, we conform to a harmonic scale.

We use a double-stranded Perfect Fifth scale, based on the ideal text size of 16px and a secondary important number of 14px. We round the values to the nearest pixel for sanity. That gives us the following scale:

Pixels Ems
54 3.375
47 2.953
36 2.25
32 1.969
24 1.5
21 1.313
16 1
14 0.875
11 0.667
