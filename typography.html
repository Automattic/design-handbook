Typography

WordPress is all about spreading ideas, freedom of speech, specifically through the written word. On this page, we'll explore the guidelines for consistent typography on WordPress.com

Typefaces

WordPress.com uses two typefaces: Open Sans and Merriweather. These two faces, if used correctly, should be another vital element of our design language, giving a sense of familiarity to the users.

Open Sans

Open Sans is a humanist sans serif typeface designed by Steve Matteson, Type Director of Ascender Corp. Designed with an upright stress, open forms and a neutral, yet friendly appearance. It was optimized for print, web, and mobile interfaces, and has excellent legibility characteristics in its letterforms. It has inspired us greatly and the name itself aligns well with our values as a company. This typeface has gotten increasingly popular, so we might consider a more tailored approach in the future. But for now, Open Sans is queen: it's in our Logotype, it's our text face.

Merriweather

This gracious typeface by Eben Sorkin started being used after very many typesetting explorations. Eventually we all agreed Merriweather was the winner. Merriweather features a very large x height, slightly condensed letterforms, a mild diagonal stress, sturdy serifs and open forms. It should be in all titles and potentially in some blockquotes.

Weights and Styles

We are very specific as to what weights to use of our glorious typefaces. Only in this way can we assure that our design language is consistent. We use multiple weights from Open Sans: Light, Regular, Semi-bold, and Bold. As for Merriweather, we stick to the Bold weight.

Typographic Modular Scale

An harmonic ratio helps in creating a more harmonious design and also helps standardize stuff. If we use the same scale across WordPress.com, things feel more cohesive - it's as much about consistency as it is about harmony: instead of using arbitrary numbers, we conform to a harmonic scale.
